Side Quest

The Netsuke Case

A Matter of Personal Honor
The Situation

You have stumbled upon something entirely unrelated to the murder of William Thoreson Sr. — and yet, somehow, equally urgent.

Rupert Thoreson, Will's twin brother and the estate's unofficial photographer, is the proud owner of a remarkable collection of Japanese Netsuke — hand-carved ivory and boxwood toggles from the Edo period, each one a miniature work of art. Frogs. Dragons. Merchants. Monks. Each one small enough to fit in a closed fist. Each one irreplaceable.

The collection lives in a hand-lacquered case that Rupert acquired in Kyoto in 1921. It is one of his most prized possessions.

The case is locked.

The key is missing.

Rupert has no idea where it is. He last saw it Tuesday. He is too proud to say how much this is bothering him. You can tell anyway.

"I'm not saying it's important. The case isn't going anywhere. I just — I'd like to know where it is. That's all. If anyone happens to see a small brass key, perhaps slightly worn, with a loop at the top — no rush. Truly. No rush at all."

— Rupert Thoreson, pretending he is fine

The Quest

Somewhere on the premises this evening, a small brass key has gone missing. It could be anywhere. A coat pocket. A side table. Wedged between sofa cushions. Sitting on a windowsill where someone set it down without thinking.

This quest has nothing to do with the murder. The detectives currently investigating William Thoreson's death have, understandably, bigger things on their minds. Which means this is entirely up to you.

Find the key. Return it to Rupert. Try not to make a big deal of it — he'll be embarrassed enough as it is.

What You're Looking For

A small brass key. Slightly worn. Loop at the top. Approximately the size of a large coat button. It may or may not have a faded red ribbon tied through the loop — Rupert used to use one to keep track of it, but he's not sure if the ribbon is still attached.

You'll know it when you see it. It looks like it belongs to something important.

Upon Completion
The Eternal Gratitude of Rupert Thoreson

and a very firm handshake.

This quest is not canon. It will not help you solve the murder.
It will, however, make one man very happy.
