The Announcement

Stand where everyone can see you. Take a breath.

"Thank you all for coming tonight."

Look around the room.

"As many of you know, my father invited you here for a dinner party. He said he had an announcement to make — something that would 'change everything.'"

"Most of you know my father. William Thoreson. He built this family from nothing — came to Dallas in 1899 with very little and spent the next thirty years turning it into something."

"Thoreson Toys. Started in a workshop on Commerce Street in 1902. By 1920 it was one of the most successful toy manufacturers in the Southwest. By 1930, his name was on buildings."

"He was a self-made man. That's how he told it. That's how everyone told it."

Longer pause. Let confusion set in.

"My father... William Thoreson... is dead."

Let that land. Wait for reactions.

"He was found earlier this evening. I don't know how. I don't know why. The authorities have been contacted, but due to the storm, we're on our own for now."

"No one leaves until we understand what happened."

"I'm Scott Thoreson — his eldest son. I've handled this family's legal affairs for years. I never imagined I'd be investigating my own father's death."

"But here we are. And I need your help."

The Rules

More businesslike now — still in character, but organizing.

"Here's how this will work."

"We'll investigate in phases. After each phase, I'll call everyone back together to share what we've found."

"Some rooms are open now. Others are closed — if a door is closed, it's off limits for now."

"You'll see blue tape on certain items. Those are personal belongings. Please respect them."

"Some of you may have particular expertise. If you find something you can't make sense of, look for someone who can. Some documents require specific knowledge to interpret."

"If you find a document with a QR code, scan it with your app — but you'll need the right skill. Find the person with the right expertise."

"If you want to search another guest — and you might — come to me first. We'll handle it properly."

"The goal is simple: find out what happened to my father."

"Let's start by learning what we can about him. His business. His dealings. Anything that might explain why someone would want him dead."

Final look around.

"The Living Room, Dining Room, Parlor, Kitchen, Breakfast Nook, and my father's Office are all open."

"Begin."
